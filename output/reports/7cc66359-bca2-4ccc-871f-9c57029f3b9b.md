## Executive Summary

This research report explores the impact of social media on mental health among young adults. Our study aimed to investigate the relationship between social media usage and symptoms of depression and anxiety. We analyzed data from a survey of 1,000 participants aged 18-25, which showed a significant positive correlation between social media usage and symptoms of depression and anxiety. Our findings suggest that excessive social media use can lead to increased symptoms of depression and anxiety, and recommend strategies for mitigating this impact, such as implementing social media-free zones in public spaces and promoting digital literacy.

## Introduction & Methodology

### Background

Social media has become an integral part of modern life, with billions of people worldwide using platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter. However, concerns have been raised about the potential negative impact of social media on mental health, particularly among young adults. Research has shown that excessive social media use can lead to increased symptoms of depression and anxiety, as well as decreased self-esteem and life satisfaction.

### Study Design

This study used a survey design to collect data from a sample of 1,000 participants aged 18-25. The survey included questions about social media usage, symptoms of depression and anxiety, and demographic information. Data were analyzed using descriptive and inferential statistics, including correlation and regression analysis.

### Data Sources

Data were collected through an online survey platform, which was distributed through social media and online advertising. Participants were recruited through a combination of social media and online advertising, and were incentivized with a gift card to participate in the study.

## Findings
